Taitu

My name is Taitu Ustanny, I currently attend Henry Snyder High School in Jersey City. I started attending Snyder in the fall of 2018 when I moved to the United States. Before moving to the US I lived in Kingston, Jamaica where I attended an all girls high school. With my life changing so much in the past year came many different unexpected stresses and difficulties, but everyday I try my best to embrace and experience all that life has to offer. My difficult experiences in regards to mental health has taught me to embrace change and keep positive even in the most stressful situations. I know first-hand how challenging adjusting to major life changes is and I try to support those around me who are having a hard time. I decided to apply to the Girls Who Code Summer Immersion Program because I have always had an avid interest in Computer Science and technology in general, I thought that participating in this program would give me the kick start in Computer Science that I needed, and it did! In the future want to pursue a career in Computer Science and combine it with my passion for art.

Ruth

My name is Ruth Itua. I'm a senior at Sayreville War Memorial High School. Mental Health is a topic that I'm really passionate about and believe to be important in our fast-paced society. By becoming educated on this topic, I believe the human race will not only become a stronger unit but also individuals will be able to attain a better mental state of being. Additionally, I believe that awareness will be an indirect way of reducing the rapidly growing rates of suicide in this country. By creating this website, I believe that I will be able to show those going through such issues that they have people rooting for them and that they are not going through the process alone. In the future, I hope to minor in CS and major in biochemistry with the goal of interlinking both of my passions into a useful career.

Jennifer

Hello, my name is Jennifer Magana and I am an upcoming Junior at New Brunswick Highschool. I play on the girls varsity soccer team and do spring / winter track. Throughout the school year join a bunch of after school activities such as chess club, National Honors Society, Homework club and the civic engagement club. Since I always have a busy schedule, it is very important to take care of myself. I've learned this the hard way by stretching myself very thin. I wasn't taking care of myself for a long time and I ended up being overwhelmed everyday. This project allowed me to bring awareness of how important it is to take care of yourself mentally. Especially since this a topic that most people do not feel comfortable talking about. When I grow up I want to help as many people I can. This program as helped me figure out ways of how I can touch so many people at once.
